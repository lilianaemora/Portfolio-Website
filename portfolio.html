PROFESSIONAL Work

THE CLARION

The Pulse: Student Media

Nov.2022-June.2023

This was my first major professional graphic design project. I was hired by The Pulse at my college, Messiah University, as the Yearbook Manager. With the Exception of the photography, the copy for the letter from the president and the letter from the editor I made the book in its entirety. All 200 pages and the cover were designed and laid out by me. The project required coordination with 20+ offices and school organizations, parents and the student body in order to procure materials and create a keepsake commemorating the graduating class of 2023. The inspiration for this yearbook’s theme was the material culture and heritage of messiah university, particularly its architecture. My goal was to create a yearbook that reflected the rich heritage of the school and deviated visually from recent yearbook projects. The illustration on the cover is of Old Main, the building that held the bible college that would eventually become Messiah University. The stars are derived from the iron star bolts on the outside of the building that can be found on many historic buildings in Pennsylvania.

SWINGING BRIDGE MAGAZINE

The Pulse: Student Media

Nov.2022-June.2023

While working as the yearbook manager, I also designed layouts for my school’s student run magazine The Swinging Bridge. My favorite work was my spread for the article “Life Imitates Art”. The article spoke to students and professors about how they felt about difficult or unsavory topics being depicted in art or media and how to broach those topics in academic settings. To illustrate this idea, I selected artworks that depicted unsavory topics- Second World War HIROSHIMA from the AP/Stanley Troutman; Abduction of a Sabine Woman by Giambologna; 3rd of May 1880 by Francisco Goya; and Self Portrait with the Spanish Flu by Edvard Munch.
